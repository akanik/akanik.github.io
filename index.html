About

Throughout my 15 years in journalism I have been a developer, a reporter and an editor. Each role has helped me develop skillsvthat span all aspects of data journalism, including:

  • writing and editing data stories,
  • designing and developing custom story layouts,
  • scraping websites,
  • writing well-documented code,
  • analyzing data,
  • creating data-driven visualizations and
  • submitting FOIA requests

I have also had the privilege to work for news organizations big and small, for profit and nonprofit. This has deepened my understanding of how to use data and visuals to impact different communities.

I've always enjoyed teaching data journalism and discussing how technology can improve news. Since my first NICAR conference in 2014, I've been an annual speaker. Here are some of the past sessions I've facilitiated:

  • Scripted mapping (2025)
  • Make mapping better (2024)
  • How and when to open-source your code and analysis (2023)
  • Mapping for analysis and online display (2022)

In addition to working with data, I also enjoy baking, biking and gardening.
